About Me

Hello! I'm Maleek McKenzie, a passionate advocate and the Vice Chair of the Conservation Committee for the Sierra Club's Oregon Chapter. My work is driven by the belief that we must be the change we want to see in the world. From my time as an Electrical Engineering student to my current leadership role, I've focused on tangible action. In 2013, my dedication to service was recognized with a campus Humanitarian Award.

My experience spans from grassroots organizing on climate action and voter registration drives with NYPIRG to direct advocacy against corporate pollution and engaging with elected officials. I specialize in coalition building, policy analysis, and mobilizing public support for critical causes. I'm always eager to connect with like-minded individuals and organizations to build a better future together.

Community Rebuilding & Food Security

Service Learning in New Orleans

Unlike traditional volunteering, my week-long service learning trip to New Orleans involved both direct community service and structured reflection. We didn't just provide labor; we engaged in daily discussions about the systemic issues behind the challenges we were witnessing, such as environmental injustice, food deserts, and the long-term impacts of Hurricane Katrina. This approach combines hands-on work with critical thinking, fostering a deeper understanding of how we, as individuals, can contribute to meaningful, long-term solutions beyond just volunteering our time.

Human Rights & Global Awareness

My Role & Responsibilities

As a student activist and President of the International Student Activist Organization for 2 years, I worked with other advocates to spread awareness of human trafficking. A key part of this campaign involved organizing and leading a march through the town of Canton, hosting campus events where we engaged in knowledge sharing and a keynote speech from Gabriel Bol Deng, one of the "Lost Boys of Sudan". Through our collective efforts we were able to engage not just the campus community but also residents of the village and students from nearby universities such as St. Lawrence University, SUNY Potsdam, and Clarkson University.

Hunger & Houselessness

My Role & Responsibilities

I participated in and helped lead campus and community-based awareness projects to highlight the crisis of houselessness affecting multiple populations, including youth, veterans, and members of the LGBTQ+ community. This work included organizing a "Sleep Out" event that garnered local news coverage to raise awareness and creating powerful visual displays to share statistics, aiming to foster empathy and drive support for local shelters and support services.

Environmental & Climate Justice

Advocacy in Action: Opposing Zenith Energy

As part of my work with the Sierra Club, I played a role in the campaign urging the Oregon Department of Environmental Quality (DEQ) to deny Zenith Energy's air quality permit. Our formal submission highlighted the public health risks to the local Environmental Justice community and cited Zenith's history of non-compliance and dishonesty.

Opposing the 'Fix Our Forests Act' (FOFA)

I authored and submitted a Letter to the Editor arguing against FOFA, outlining how the bill prioritizes logging interests over community protection, bypasses critical environmental laws like the Endangered Species Act (ESA) and NEPA, and threatens public health by potentially worsening wildfire smoke. As a lifelong asthmatic, I emphasized the personal health risks posed by poor air quality.

Testimony Against PGE's Harborton Project & UGB Expansion

I have delivered public testimony against Portland General Electric's (PGE) Harborton Reliability Project, raising concerns about its environmental impact. Additionally, I have testified against the proposed Urban Growth Boundary (UGB) expansion, advocating for smarter, denser growth to protect our farmlands and natural areas.

Civic & Political Engagement

My Role & Responsibilities

A core part of my activism involves ensuring that communities have a voice in our democracy. As a project leader and later as an elected, student Board Member for NYPIRG, I led numerous voter registration drives on campus. This work extended to advocating for affordable higher education and engaging directly with elected officials, to discuss policy and community needs. As a board member, I was responsible for voting on the official stances and campaigns that the organization would pursue statewide.
